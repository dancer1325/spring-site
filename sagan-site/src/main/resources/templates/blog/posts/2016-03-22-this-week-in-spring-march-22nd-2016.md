# This Week in Spring - March 22nd, 2016

_Engineering | Josh Long |  March 22, 2016 | 2 Comments_

Welcome to another installation of *This Week in Spring*! As usual, we've got a lot to cover so let's get to it! This week I'm supposed to be in Paris *and* Belgium. Unfortunately, because of [the recent, tragic, disgusting and cowardly attacks on the Brussels airport and metro](http://edition.cnn.com/2016/03/22/europe/brussels-explosions/), I am not entirely sure about whether I'll be in Belgium physically. Tonight's meetup talk, however, *will* carry on remotely, [via streaming](https://twitter.com/vedw/status/712235052697034752). Stay tuned to [my Twitter handle - @starbuxman](http://twitter.com/starbuxman) for more.

That said, there's a lot of great content so let's get to it!

-   Spring Data lead Oliver Gierke has just [announced the first release candidate of Spring Data Hopper](https://spring.io/blog/2016/03/18/first-release-candidate-of-spring-data-hopper-released).
-   our local Spring and Kotlin ninja Sebastien Deleuze has put together a blog [demonstrating some interesting applications of Spring and Kotlin](https://spring.io/blog/2016/03/20/a-geospatial-messenger-with-kotlin-spring-boot-and-postgresql) in his sample application, a *GeoSpatial Messenger*.
-   Spring Security lead Rob Winch [has announced Spring Security SAML 1.0.2](https://spring.io/blog/2016/03/21/spring-security-saml-1-0-2-released).
-   Spring Tool Suite lead Martin Lippert continues his series [on using Spring Boot and Cloud Foundry from the Spring Tool Suite](https://spring.io/blog/2016/03/22/the-spring-boot-dashboard-in-sts-part-4-working-with-cloud-foundry-manifest-files). This installment looks at Cloud Foundry manifests, among other things.
-   Spring Security and Spring Session lead Rob Winch has just announced two new cuts: [Spring Session 1.2.0.RC1](https://spring.io/blog/2016/03/17/spring-session-1-2-0-rc1-released) and [Spring Session 1.1.1](https://spring.io/blog/2016/03/17/spring-session-1-1-1-released).
-   Ashely Puls of New Relic does a JVM deep dive in her SpringOne2GX 2015 talk - [What it means to be just in time](http://spring.io/blog/2016/03/22/springone2gx-2015-replay-what-it-means-to-be-just-in-time)
-   Matt Raible's Epic framework smackdown talk is back hear him [compare ReactJS, AngularJS, EmberJS](http://spring.io/blog/2016/03/22/springone2gx-2015-replay-comparing-hot-javascript-frameworks-angularjs-ember-js-and-react-js)
-   Michael Ploed from InnQ discusses [Building microservices with event sourcing and CQRS](http://spring.io/blog/2016/03/22/springone2gx-2015-replay-building-microservices-with-event-sourcing-and-cqrs) form SpringOne2GX 2015
-   Spring Boot strike you as being too *magical*? It's not. It's just an abstraction, and it's easy to reason about if you understand the abstraction. Our pal Siva Prasad Reddy has put together a nice post that [pulls back the curtain around how Spring Boot's auto-configuration works](http://www.sivalabs.in/2016/03/how-springboot-autoconfiguration-magic.html)
-   our pals from Heroku are back at it again with another great post on using Spring Cloud, [this time on using Spring Cloud's Zuul integration](https://blog.heroku.com/archives/2016/3/2/using_netflix_zuul_to_proxy_your_microservices).
-   our pal Biju Kunjummen has a nice [post on deploying Spring Boot applications with Cloud Foundry](https://dzone.com/articles/deploying-spring-boot)
-   I think a lot of people have questions about how to write effective service [clients using the Spring Cloud Hystrix circuit breaker integration, and - amazingly - this documentation does a pretty good job](https://docs.pivotal.io/spring-cloud-services/circuit-breaker/writing-client-applications.html)!
-   I'll [be speaking at the Darmstadt JUG April 21st, come join me](http://www.jug-da.de/2016/04/Spring-Cloud/)!
-   Take a look at what's coming in JHipster 3.0, [complete with deep Spring Cloud integration!](http://www.ipponusa.com/blog/jhipster-3-0-introducing-microservices/)
