# This Week in Spring - February 14th, 2024

_Engineering | Josh Long |  February 13, 2024 | 0 Comments_

Hi, Spring fans! Welcome to another installment of *This Week in Spring*! Friends, tomorrow is Valentine's day, and I *love* Spring. So, it's a very exciting thing indeed to be able to share this week's jam-packed roundup. Let's dive right into it!

-   [Spring Tools 4.21.1 is now available](https://spring.io/blog/2024/02/08/spring-tools-4-21-1-released)
-   In the latest installment of a *Bootiful Podcast*, I talk to the good, the [great Dr. Venkat Subramaniam](https://spring.io/blog/2024/02/08/a-bootiful-podcast-dr-venkat-subramaniam-on-the-latest-in-java)
-   In this installment of Spring Tips, [I look at the new Spring AI project](https://spring.io/blog/2024/02/08/spring-tips-spring-ai)
-   In this installment of Spring Tips, [I look at the new Spring Boot Testjars project](https://spring.io/blog/2024/02/08/spring-tips-spring-boot-testjars)
-   our friend Siva has a nice video on using [Java 17 and 21 with Spring Boot and Testcontainers](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q2LSz7cnC1g)
-   I loved working with the Flowable team to bring AOT native [image support to their project, and its' finally landing](https://github.com/flowable/flowable-engine/pull/3831/commits)
-   ACM Members: the "Spring Boot in Practice" audiobook, which is a cookbook-style guide to Spring application development, is now available. [I love this book and wrote the foreword for it. Check it out!](https://share.percipio.com/cd/rrhRsVV_z)
-   I did a talk looking at [Spring Boot 3 at VOXXED Days CERN and you might like it](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8l0tv3_jFoY)
-   Baeldung has yet another interesting article, this one looking at custom JSON serialization and deserialization with [Spring's reactive `WebClient`](https://www.baeldung.com/spring-webclient-json-custom-deserialization)
-   You've heard of OpenRewrite, surely. There are a bunch of great features there to make it easy to write programmatic recipes to transform code. Moderne, the company behind it, has jst written a nice blog showing how they used [the OpenRewrite project to automatically upgrade their own Spring Boot project to Spring Boot 3.2.](https://www.moderne.io/blog/how-moderne-engineering-automated-its-spring-boot-3-migration?hss_channel=lis-5EUCsrnPJv)
