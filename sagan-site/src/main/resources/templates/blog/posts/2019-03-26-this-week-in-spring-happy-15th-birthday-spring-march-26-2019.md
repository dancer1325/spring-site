# This Week in Spring (Happy 15th Birthday Spring!) - March 26, 2019

_Engineering | Josh Long |  March 26, 2019 | 3 Comments_

Hi Spring fans! Welcome to another installment of *This Week in Spring*! I'm in Pleasanton, CA, visiting one of Pivotal's large customers, and then tomorrow it's off to New York City for my talk there at the Kafka Summit with the [excellent Tim Berglund](http://twitter.com/tlberglund) and [James Watters](http://twitter.com/WattersJames). Join us! Then, next week it's off to South Africa (for the SpringOne Tour shows in [Capetown](https://springonetour.io/2019/cape-town) and [Johannesburg](https://springonetour.io/2019/johannesburg)) and then it's off [to Mauritius for the DevConf](https://conference.mscc.mu/speaker/f2617bc2-d35e-4fd7-9cea-02060f3e1608) - if you're in any of those places, [don't hesitate to say hi!](http://twitter.com/Starbuxman).

Also, totally worth including that on Sunday Spring Framework marked the 15th anniversary of its release on the 25th of March, 2004! Happy 15th birthday, Spring!

-   Interesting insight from Roy T. Fielding, the creator of REST and one of the major contributors to HTTP itself, on what "REST" is: "I disagree that the popular understanding of the term is different. What is different is the desire of certain companies, authors, [and speakers to say REST when they know it is just HTTP; not because they don't know the meaning](https://twitter.com/fielding/status/1108092713516163072). Put another way: don't say "REST" when you're not using things like hypermedia (HATEOAS). I need to do better here, too.
