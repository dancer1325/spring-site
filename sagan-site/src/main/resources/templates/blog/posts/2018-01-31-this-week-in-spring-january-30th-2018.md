# This Week in Spring - January 30th, 2018

_Engineering | Josh Long |  January 31, 2018 | 3 Comments_

Hi Spring fans! Welcome to another installment of *This Week in Spring*! This week I was in Los Angeles and Chicago and now I'm in San Francisco, taking meetings with Pivotal ecosystem folks and customers. We've got an incredibly busy week's worth of stuff ahead of us, so let's get to it!

-   Reactor project lead Stephane Maldini has announced [that Reactor Bismuth-SR5](https://spring.io/blog/2018/01/30/reactor-bismuth-sr5-is-out) is out with fixes, Reactive Streams 1.0.2, Netty 4.1.20 and a few new cool features there
-   Check out [this post](https://spring.io/blog/2018/01/30/next-generation-oauth-2-0-support-with-spring-security) by Spring Security ninja Joe Grandja on what the future of OAuth 2 support in Spring is
-   Spring Security lead Rob Winch has just announced an immediate update for [a CVE for Spring Security 5.0.1, 4.2.4, and 4.1.5](https://spring.io/blog/2018/01/30/cve-2017-8030-spring-security-5-0-1-4-2-4-4-1-5-released)
-   Reactive Spring web ninja Sebastien Deleuze has just announced the integration and support for Reactive Smile (binary JSON) [support in Spring Framework 5.0.4](https://jira.spring.io/browse/SPR-16151)
-   Vedran Pavić has [announced Spring Session 2.0.1](https://spring.io/blog/2018/01/25/spring-session-2-0-1-released)
-   Spring Framework lead Juergen Hoeller has just [announced that Spring Framework 5.0.3 and 4.3.14 available now](https://spring.io/blog/2018/01/23/spring-framework-5-0-3-and-4-3-14-available-now)
-   [James Williams](http://twitter.com/cloudfoundry) does a great job looking at the form and function of buildpacks. [This is a very good, and quick, watch](https://twitter.com/cloudfoundry/status/958451939733266432).
-   A friendly reminder: we try to help, [wherever and whenever possible](https://twitter.com/springcentral/status/958448315212836865). We're happy to see some of you appreciate that :-) If you reach out to the various Spring channels on Twitter ([Spring Data](http://twitter.com/SpringData), [Spring Security](http://twitter.com/SpringSecurity), [Spring Boot](http://twitter.com/SpringBoot), [Spring Cloud](http://twitter.com/SpringCloud), [Spring Framework](http://twitter.com/Springframework), [SpringCentral](http://twitter.com/SpringCentral) or [yours truly](http://twitter.com/Starbuxman) you'll probably get a response. You might also try the various Gitter.im chat rooms for [the Spring projects](http://gitter.im/spring-projects) and [Spring Cloud](http://gitter.im/Spring-Cloud) in particular. Also, \[we keep a robust [presence on StackOverflow, as well](http://spring.io/questions).
