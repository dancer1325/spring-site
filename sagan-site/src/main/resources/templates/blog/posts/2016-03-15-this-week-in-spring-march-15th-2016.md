# This Week in Spring - March 15th, 2016

_Engineering | Josh Long |  March 15, 2016 | 0 Comments_

Welcome to another installment of *This Week in Spring!* What a crazy week it's been since we last talked! I spent a day in San Francisco, Germany, Scotland, and Romania between Monday and Friday last. It's nice this week to be in (warm!) southern California in Los Angeles and San Diego speaking to customers and at a couple of meetups! If you're around, I hope you'll join me for [the Cloud Native Meetup here in Los Angeles](http://www.meetup.com/Los-Angeles-Java-User-Group/events/228563209/) (tonight!).

-   the [2016 NFJS tour has just kicked off](https://nofluffjuststuff.com/home/main), first in Minneapolis. The amazing [Craig Walls](http://twitter.com/habuma) and [Matt Stine](http://twitter.com/mstine) be presenting at most of the NFJS shows in 2016 on all manner of topics including Spring Boot, Spring Cloud, Spring Security, Spring Data, Spring Cloud Data Flow, and (later this year) Spring’s new reactive stuff. Make sure to check out the event in your city!
-   JUnit 5 is coming! [Check out Lukas Eder's quick runthrough of some of the new features in JUnit 5](https://dzone.com/articles/junit-5-basics?edition=153261&utm_source=Spotlight&utm_medium=email&utm_content=queue&utm_campaign=java%202016-03-15). JUnit 5 is critical infrastructure code, and it's for this reason that we at Pivotal were happy to help sponsor some of its development.
-   Get the [details on the new JEP for type inference in Java](https://dzone.com/articles/java-as-new-local-variable-type-inference)
-   Spring Integration lead [Gary Russell has just announced Spring for Apache Kafka M1](https://spring.io/blog/2016/03/14/spring-for-apache-kafka-milestone-1-available)
-   Every now and then people say Spring Boot has *too much magic*. (Invariably, others [will complain it has too much boilerplate](https://twitter.com/phillip_webb/status/705909774001377280)!) I think the key to demystifying Spring Boot's capabilities is to understand its secret sauce, auto-confiuration, which is *very* capably explained [in this epic blog](http://www.sivalabs.in/2016/03/how-springboot-autoconfiguration-magic.html) by our pal K. Siva Prasad Reddy. Also, undestand that you can start the application with `--debug=true` and you'll see an enumeration of which auto-configuration conditionals are in play.
-   project Reactor lead Stephane Maldini talks [about how Reactor fits in with the larger reactive theme of Spring 5](https://spring.io/blog/2016/03/11/reactor-core-2-5-becomes-a-unified-reactive-foundation-on-java-8)
-   make sure to join [Ratpack contributor Danny Hyun for an upcoming webinar introducing Ratpack and Spring Boot](https://spring.io/blog/2016/03/09/webinar-spring-boot-and-ratpack-web-framework)
-   Make sure to join Spring Cloud Stream lead Marius Bogoevici [for a webinar on stream processing in the cloud](https://spring.io/blog/2016/03/09/webinar-stream-processing-in-the-cloud-with-data-microservices)
-   the incomparable [Gunnar Hillert wrote a blog on how to use `.jsp` pages with embedded Spring Boot applications](http://hillert.blogspot.com/2016/03/spring-boot-with-jsp-in-executable-jar.html). He does this *mainly* to support migrating an existing application; *not* recommended for new applications!
-   want to write [an Atlassian Connect application using Spring Boot](https://developer.atlassian.com/blog/2016/03/connecting-connect-with-spring-boot/)?
-   Apress has [just published *Pro Spring Boot*](http://www.apress.com/9781484214329) - check it out!
-   Don't miss Sebastian Deleuze's SpringOne2GX 2015 replay on [Isomorphic templating with Spring Boot, Nashorn and React](http://spring.io/blog/2016/03/15/springone2gx-2015-replay-isomorphic-templating-with-spring-boot-nashorn-and-react)
-   Monish Unni, formerly of ETrade, talking about distributed call tracing in his SpringOne2GX 2015 talk [Introducing CallTracing(tm), based on RabbitMQ, Spring and Zipkin](http://spring.io/blog/2016/03/15/springone2gx-2015-replay-introducing-calltracing-tm-based-on-rabbitmq-spring-and-zipkin)
-   Joseph Paulchell from CapitalOne plays with Analytics, CEP and Spring XD in his [SpringOne2GX 2015 replay](http://spring.io/blog/2016/03/15/supercharging-operations-and-analytics-using-spring-xd-to-support-analytics-and-cep)
