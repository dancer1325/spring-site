# This Week in Spring - April 29th, 2015

_Engineering | Josh Long |  April 28, 2015 | 0 Comments_

Welcome to another installment of *This Week in Spring*! This week, I'm in Barcelona, Spain for the [Spring I/O conference](http://springio.net).

![](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/CDs-ekvVEAAwKdH.jpg)![](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/CDs-eqHVAAAoizY.jpg)

(can you spot \[your favorite Spring team\](http://spring.io/team) or community member?)

-   Spring Social lead Craig Walls just announced the [release of Spring Social Facebook 2.0.1](https://spring.io/blog/2015/04/27/spring-social-facebook-2-0-1-released), the first maintenance release in the Spring Social Facebook 2.0 line.
-   the good Dr. Syer just published a [blog on different ways to bind services in Cloud Foundry](https://spring.io/blog/2015/04/27/binding-to-data-services-with-spring-boot-in-cloud-foundry)
-   Spring Security lead Rob Winch [just released Spring Security 4.0.1](https://spring.io/blog/2015/04/23/spring-security-4-0-1-released), the first maintenance release in the Spring Security 4.0 line
-   This Week's SpringOne2GX Replays are out! Marius Bogoevici shows us how to [Build highly modular and testable business systems with Spring Integration](http://spring.io/blog/2015/04/28/springone2gx-2014-replay-building-highly-modular-and-testable-business-systems-with-spring-integration), Brian Cavalier talks about [Efficient Client-Server Communication with Differential Synchronization and JSON Patch](https://spring.io/blog/2015/04/28/springone2gx-2014-replay-efficient-client-server-communication-with-differential-synchronization-and-json-patch) and Topher Bullock and Will Tran review [Server-side JavaScript with Nashorn and Spring](http://spring.io/blog/2015/04/28/springone2gx-2014-replay-server-side-javascript-with-nashorn-and-spring).
-   Were you at JAX London last week? No? Then you missed *Domain Driven Design* author [Eric Evan's talk on bounded contexts with microservices](https://jaxenter.de/jax-2015-keynote-eric-evans-ddd-microservices-18605). Check it out!
-   Zurelsoft's Sanjay Shah put together a decent, [high-level pitch for Spring Boot](http://blog.zurelsoft.com/blog/what-will-get-you-to-adopt-spring-boot-for-spring-based-applications)
-   [Eberhard Wolff](http://twitter.com/ewolff)'s just [perfectly expressed](https://twitter.com/ewolff/status/592673619496538114) the inertia behind monolithic applications: "Monolithic gravity: A large system will attract new features and changes. In the process it will become larger and eventually collapse."
-   My pal Geertjan, who works on Oracle's Netbeans platform, just introduced a very cool contribution from [Aggelos Karalias](https://twitter.com/mehieltwit) that integrates [autocompletion support for Spring Boot's metadata about configuration properties](https://blogs.oracle.com/geertjan/entry/new_spring_boot_integration_for). This support is similar to what's available in the latest STS and IntelliJ IDEA 14.1 releases. Very cool!
-   [InfoQ](http://infoq.com)'s just published a [nice post on Spring Security 4](http://www.infoq.com/news/2015/04/spring-security-4).
-   Apache Geode (the core of Pivotal's Gemfire distributed datagrid technology) has just passed a vote [to be an incubator project in the Apache Software Foundation](https://www.mail-archive.com/general@incubator.apache.org/msg48427.html)! Congratulations!
-   Our pal [David Welch](http://twitter.com/david_welch) just shared a [JDBC-backed `MetricRepository`](https://github.com/Lemniscate/spring-metrics-jdbc) that looks mighty useful! Check it out!
