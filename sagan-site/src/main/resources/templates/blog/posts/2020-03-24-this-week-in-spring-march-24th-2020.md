# This Week in Spring - March 24th, 2020

_Engineering | Josh Long |  March 24, 2020 | 0 Comments_

Hi, Spring fans! Welcome to another installment of *This Week in Spring*! It's a beautiful Tuesday for any number of reasons. I presented in the [DevDotNext digital edition show just a few hours ago](https://www.devdotnext.com/digital) - that was a *lot* of fun and I highly recommend you consider attending the next editions (online or - assuming we get past this pandemic - online).

Today is *also* the 16th birthday of Spring Framework 1.0 - released this day in 2004! What a journey! And of course, the person who announced that that version, the amazing [Thomas Risberg (@trisberg)](http://twitter.com/trisberg), is one of many people who were there then and are still here on the Spring team now. Spring's come a long way since then! [Check out the blog itself](https://spring.io/blog/2004/03/24/spring-framework-1-0-final-released). If you want to see the original blog in all of its early-2000s glory, it's here on the [Internet Wayback machine](https://web.archive.org/web/20040330131500/http://www.springframework.org/).

I mentioned the birthday on [Twitter](https://twitter.com/starbuxman/status/1242353917691719680) and got some nice replies about people's use of the Framework in the replies. I love this sort of stuff. :-)

Anyway, let's get to this week's roundup. There is a *ton* of stuff to get to! I've been doing this roundup every week since January 2011, and there's *always* something interesting to review. As there had been for the six years before that since the 1.0 release. What a wonderful problem to have :)
