# This Week in Spring - June 10th, 2025

_Engineering | Josh Long |  June 10, 2025 | 1 Comment_

Hi, Spring fans! Welcome to another installment of *This Week in Spring*! It's been a busy week indeed since we last spoke! Last week I was in Amsterdam for the IntelliJ IDEA conference and for the JSpring event in Utrecht. Now, I'm in Tokyo, Japan, for the JJUG Spring 2025 event. Importantly: both of these are Java events that take place on or around the spring season in the northern hemisphere, and are not (just?) about Spring Framework. Funnily enough, though, I did get a kick out of seeing this article of Spring creator Rod Johnson presenting [at JSpring in Utrecth 20 years ago!](https://biplatform.nl/magazines/Aveq/109362.pdf). The more things change...

Anyway my friends, we've got a ton of stuff to dive into this week so let's do.

-   In last week's installment of *A Bootiful Podcast*, I talked to [IntelliJ IDEA lead Aleksey Stuaklov](https://spring.io/blog/2025/06/05/a-bootiful-podcast-aleksey-stukalov)
-   InfoQ has a nice look at Spring creator Rod Johnson's new Embabel framework, which is a framework [for building agentic systems and services and which builds on Spring AI](https://www.infoq.com/news/2025/06/introducing-embabel-ai-agent/)
-   seven tips to improve [the startup time for your Spring Boot applications](https://medium.com/@ravibiswas0909/spring-boot-startup-secrets-7-tricks-to-make-your-application-launch-in-seconds-b711186201cb)
-   Interesting! The GraalVM team are working on a new effort, [*Project Crema*](https://t.co/4YwBr8GkVS), which lets you dynamically load and evaluate classes at runtime in a native image.
-   Interesting! [RAG with Java and Spring AI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=RIQGv3u79xw)
-   also: I wrote a post looking at how to build [*Production-worthy AI with Spring AI 1.0*](https://blogs.vmware.com/tanzu/production-worthy-ai-with-spring-ai-1-0/)
-   A nice Japanese-language post introducing [Spring AI's support for building Model-Context Protocol services](https://qiita.com/yosukenaruse/items/f9f218106d11267cd2ec)

-   Here's another nice [piece on Spring creator Rod Johnson's new agentic framework, Embabel](https://www.infoworld.com/article/4003278/spring-java-creator-unveils-ai-agent-framework-for-the-jvm.html)
