# This Week in Spring - February 10th, 2026

_Engineering | Josh Long |  February 10, 2026 | 0 Comments_

Hi, Spring fans! Welcome to another installment of *This Week in Spring*! It's February 10th, 2026, as I write this from lovely London, UK. I spoke at the local Java User Group here last night, had a wonderful time. Tomorrow, I'm going home. It's been fun, but it's time to conclude this roller coaster whirlwind tour of Toronto, Canada; Helsinki, Finland; Stockholm, Sweden; Istanbul, Turkey; and London, UK.

I've been busy, but as usual, the community's been busier! There's a ton to get into so let's dive right into it!

-   I did a video on how to build production-worthy AI systems and services with Spring AI. In it we look at most of what you need to know from Spring AI *and* we look at how to make it production worthy with observability, scalability, and - very importantly - security. Yes, security! We look at the new experimental Spring AI Community project [called MCP Security](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Mpd1740f2Vs) which we can use to, you guessed it, secure MCP endpoints (in this case, with OAuth).
-   another very interesting effort from our pal Thomas Vitale. It's called Docling, and it's a tool to prepare [documents for AI (using Spring AI, natch)](https://www.thomasvitale.com/ai-document-processing-docling-java-arconia-spring-boot/)
-   why AI feel hard [for Java developers, and why it doesn't have to](https://suyambuganesh.substack.com/p/why-ai-feels-hard-for-java-developers?triedRedirect=true)
-   in last week's installment of *A Bootiful Podcast*, I had the privilege of [sitting down to talk with JetBrains' Marco Behler](https://spring.io/blog/2026/02/05/a-bootiful-podcast-marco-behler)
-   a nice video on prompt [caching with Spring AI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eYb7BKW4QcU)
-   I like (but also hate LOL) this post comparing raw AI model [client library usage to Spring AI](https://medium.com/javarevisited/spring-ai-vs-raw-java-the-fastest-way-out-of-llm-boilerplate-ddd8569690d0). I like it because it does a great job making clear the start difference in verbosity and boilerplate, but I hate it because it's sad to think this was even a question that needed answering! Use Spring AI. It'll get you to production faster in every way.
-   a nice post on Piotr's techblog on [using Spring AI with external MCP servers](https://piotrminkowski.com/2026/02/06/spring-ai-with-external-mcp-servers/)
-   I've been talking to people a lot in recent weeks about - believe it or not - Spring Batch! And imagine thus my deluight in finding this series [on Spring Batch (in Korean)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i4oUiieOK88&list=PLtUgHNmvcs6oBv-Edp_jD4KZY76wT2x4X)
-   the Spring Batch plugin [for Jetbrains IntelliJ has been updated](https://plugins.jetbrains.com/plugin/18505-spring-batch)
-   nice! there's a [new Udemy course on Spring AI](https://www.udemy.com/course/spring-ai-genai/?srsltid=AfmBOorqnH240dX0IN88oQr51fv9hynNCK3Ip9GsjfwywO88bAMCNhpN)
