# This Week in Spring - 25th September, 2012

_Engineering | Josh Long |  September 26, 2012 | 0 Comments_

Welcome back to another installment of *This Week in Spring*! There's *so* much good stuff to look at this week. Can you believe we're already staring down October?

It's time to think about who or what you are going to be for halloween!

With October comes [SpringOne2GX](http://springone2gx.com), the premier event for Spring developers. Check out the [final schedule](http://springone2gx.com/conference/washington/2012/10/schedule) of [speakers and sessions](http://www.springone2gx.com/conference/washington/2012/10/sessions) for this year's event! SpringOne 2GX is your opportunity to learn from development leads and published authors on the Spring, Groovy/Grails, Tomcat and Cloud technologies.  

1.  The video from Spring Security lead Rob Winch's webinar introducing [Spring Security](http://www.springsource.org/spring-security) [is now available](http://www.springsource.org/node/3670).
2.  Oleg Zhurakousky kicks off the march to Spring Integration 2.2 with [a blog introducing the new MongoDB features in 2.2](http://blog.springsource.org/2012/09/24/whats-new-in-spring-integration-2-2-rc1-part-1-mongodb/).
3.  Gary Russell [has announced release candidate 1 of Spring Integration 2.2](http://www.springsource.org/node/3665).

4.  Costin Leau has announced [the 1.0.0.RC1 release of Spring Shell](http://www.springsource.org/node/3664), which is an extraction of the shell used in Spring Roo and made available through the Spring programming model.

5.  Roger Hughes is back at it again, this time with [a look at Spring 3.1 caching and configuration](http://java.dzone.com/articles/spring-31-caching-and-config) options, specifically focusing on setting up a `CacheManager`.
6.  The vFabric blog has an interesting look at the ["split-agent" architecture behind Spring Insight](http://blogs.vmware.com/vfabric/2012/09/spring-insight-split-agent-architecture.html), that allows the separation of the collection stage from the analysis one.
7.  Krishna Prasad has a nice post on [obtaining test coverage information for Spring Web Flow applications](http://krishnasblog.com/tag/spring-mvc/).
8.  Mattias Severson has a nice post on handling errors [in Spring MVC-based RESTful application](http://www.jayway.com/2012/09/23/improve-your-spring-rest-api-part-ii/).
9.  Ronillo Ang's put together a very brief deck on the [fundamental pieces of a Spring MVC application](http://www.slideshare.net/ronilloang/spring-mvc-demo-14356569).
10.  The JavaCode Geeks blog has a nice post [on writing persistence layers with the Spring framework](http://www.javacodegeeks.com/2012/09/spring-persistence-layer-writing.html).
11.  *L'Artech* blog has a cool post on creating [the login form for a web application using Spring MVC](http://shreymalhotra.me/blog/tutorial/spring-mvc-creating-the-homepage-part-3-web-app-series/), in part 3 of a series.
