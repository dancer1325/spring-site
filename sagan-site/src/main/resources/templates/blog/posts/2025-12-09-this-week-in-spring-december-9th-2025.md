# This Week in Spring - December 9th, 2025

_Engineering | Josh Long |  December 09, 2025 | 0 Comments_

Hi, Spring fans! Welcome to another installment of *This Week in Spring*! I am in lovely New Jersey, *the Garden State*, as I write this and I spent most of the last week in New Orleans. It's been a busy week in the Spring community and beyond and so you know what that means? There's a ton of stuff to look into! Let's dive right into it!

-   [Spring gRPC 1.0.0 is now GA!](https://spring.io/blog/2025/12/04/spring-grpc-1) Huzzah! Hooray!
-   Want to learn more about the new release direct from the Spring team? Join them, [live, for this webinar on the 11th](https://info.jetbrains.com/idea-livestream-december11-2025.html).
-   Good news, everybody, [Spring Shell 4.0.0-M2 is available now!](https://spring.io/blog/2025/12/09/spring-shell-4-0-0-m2-released).
-   [Spring AI 1.1.1 is available now](https://spring.io/blog/2025/12/05/spring-ai-1-1-1-available-now).
-   I really enjoyed this discussion with fellow Spring Developer Advocate Dan Vega on his new book *The Fundamentals of Software Engineering*, and [you can too](https://spring.io/blog/2025/12/04/a-bootiful-podcast-dan-vega).
-   Did you see this amazing Kotlin DSL for [HTMX, called KHTMX](https://wakingrufus.github.io/khtmx/spring.html)?
-   Speaking of Kotlin, have you started your journey with this amazing language? Check out this guide [on the Spring portal](https://spring.io/guides/tutorials/spring-boot-kotlin).
-   While not strictly speaking related to Spring, I think this article on Idempotency Keys - a very useful design philosophy and pattern - by fellow Java Champion Gunnar Morling is well-worth a read if you care [about building reliable distributed systems architectures](https://www.morling.dev/blog/on-idempotency-keys/).
-   It's fun looking at various people's migrations to Spring Boot 4. Here, [Piotr Mińkowski shares one](https://t.co/EFWaX8yS1E).
-   One of the nice things about Spring Boot 4 and Spring Framework 7 is the new `BeanRegistrar` mechanism, which [includes a lovely DSL in Kotlin, too](https://docs.spring.io/spring-framework/reference/core/beans/java/programmatic-bean-registration.html).
-   I love this blog from Spring Modulith [founder Oliver Drotbohm](https://odrotbohm.de/2025/12/rethinking-spring-application-integration-testing/).
