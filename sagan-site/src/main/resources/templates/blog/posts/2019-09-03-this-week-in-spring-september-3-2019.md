# This Week in Spring: September 3, 2019

_Engineering | Josh Long |  September 03, 2019 | 0 Comments_

Hi Spring fans! Welcome to another installment of *This Week in Spring*! We've just finished celebrating Labor Day here in the Long home, in San Francisco, in the US. It was a fun day spent with family and friends, and I wasn't sure if I could have any more fun but it's Tuesday, and you know what that means: another epic installment of *This Week in Spring*. I can't believe we're already in September, staring down the last few months of the year already! As usual, one of my favorite parts of the journey to the next year is the arrival of the [epic SpringOne Platform](http://springoneplatform.io) show. This year is no different: I can't wait to celebrate the show with friends in epic Austin, TX. Will you be there? [Let me know!](http://twitter.com/Starbuxman)

-   I really enjoyed last week's installment of *A Bootiful Podcast*, an interview with [Spring Cloud Data Flow legend Ilayaperumal Gopinathan](https://spring.io/blog/2019/08/30/a-bootiful-podcast-spring-cloud-data-flow-legend-ilayaperumal-gopinathan)
-   [Check out this fantastic introduction to Spring Data Neo4j RX](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nfk4782Da0E)
-   A nice article on DZone Microservices about [Contracts for Microservices With OpenAPI and Spring Cloud Contract](https://dzone.com/articles/contracts-for-microservices-with-openapi-and-sprin)
-   DZone Java has a [A Guide to Spring Framework's Annotations](https://dzone.com/articles/a-guide-to-spring-framework-annotations)
-   I also enjoyed this introduction to the concept of [autowiring in Spring](https://dzone.com/articles/autowiring-in-spring)
-   [This is interesing: Spring users will soon be able to leverage RSocket via a Netifi managed service on Alibaba Cloud](https://twitter.com/netifi_inc/status/1166770007260499970)
-   [I *love* Matt Raible's *Secure Reactive Microservices with Spring Cloud Gateway*](https://developer.okta.com/blog/2019/08/28/reactive-microservices-spring-cloud-gateway)
-   This is an interesting post by Emmanouil Gkatziouras on Dzone about how to add custom functionality [to a Spring Data repository](https://dzone.com/articles/add-custom-functionality-to-a-spring-data-reposito)
-   This is an interesting post [on creating state-machines with Spring Boot](https://dzone.com/articles/a-simple-state-machine-for-spring-boot-projects), but *not* Spring Statemachine. The post is interesting, but I'm not entirely persuaded that they couldn't have done what they needed with Spring Statemachine. Either way, interesting read.
-   Julien Dubois has published a very nice look at using R2DBC to [reactively talk to Microsoft SQL Server](https://dev.to/azure/building-a-fully-reactive-and-scalable-spring-application-with-r2dbc-and-a-serverless-database-1jke)
