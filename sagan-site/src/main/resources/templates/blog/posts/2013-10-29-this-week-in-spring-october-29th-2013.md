# This Week in Spring - October 29th, 2013

_Engineering | Josh Long |  October 29, 2013 | 0 Comments_

Welcome back to another installment of *This Week in Spring*!

If you're in the Bay Area, I hope you'll join me at 875 Howard St. in San Francisco at the Pivotal offices on the 5th floor at 5:30 both today and tomorrow. I'll be giving one talk each evening - one on building RESTful services with Spring, and the other a sort of abbreviated look at the latest and greatest in the Spring.IO platform. Also, the [SpringOne2GX 2013](http://springone2gx.com/) keynotes from [day 1](https://spring.io/blog/2013/10/02/springone2gx-2013-opening-night-keynote) and [day 2](https://spring.io/blog/2013/10/23/springone2gx-2013-day-2-keynote) are now up for your enjoyment. Make sure to check out the Day 1 keynote around 1:13:40 to hear Pivotal Labs - hardcore Ruby developers, talk about their experience with Spring and Spring Boot.

As usual, we've got a lot to cover, so let's get to it!

14.  Spring Data ninja Oliver Gierke has announced that the [Spring Data Babbage release train SR1 has been released](http://spring.io/blog/2013/10/25/spring-data-release-train-babbage-sr1-released). The release bundles a bunch of important enhancements and bug fixes and is a recommended upgrade. You can find all issues fixed in this release in our JIRA. This release train is named for [Charles Babbage](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Babbage), who was a mathematician, philosopher, inventor and mechanical engineer, who is best remembered now for originating the concept of a programmable computer.
