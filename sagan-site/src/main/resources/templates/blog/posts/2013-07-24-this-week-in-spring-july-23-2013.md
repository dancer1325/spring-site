# This Week in Spring - July 23, 2013

_Engineering | Josh Long |  July 24, 2013 | 0 Comments_

Hey everyone! Remember that SpringOne 2GX 2013 early bird expires August 9th, [so hurry](http://www.springone2gx.com/conference/santa_clara/2013/09/register) to secure the discounted rate! Also, make sure to check the agenda as new sessions have been added. This week I'm at [OSCON](http://www.oscon.com) talking to developers in the wonderful city of Portland, OR about Spring 4, REST and joining my colleagues at [Pivotal](http://gopivotal.com) to talk about Cloud Foundry, big data, and much more! If you'd like to chat, I hope you'll come to the talks that we're putting on and visit us at the Pivotal booth in the exhibition hall! It's been a big week for both Spring and Pivotal:

1.  [Pivotal HD 1.0](http://blog.gopivotal.com/products/pivotal-hd-ga), the world's fastest Hadoop distribution, was released in two flavors - Community Edition, and a Pivotal Single Node Edition (VM), a Virtual Machine download. Head over to gopivotal.com and give it a test drive - Community Edition deploys up to a 50 node cluster!
2.  We're celebrating Project Reactor's [initial milestone release](http://blog.springsource.org/2013/07/18/reactor-1-0-0-m1-a-foundation-for-asynchronous-fast-data-applications-on-the-jvm/) - 1.0.0M1 - which *already* benchmarked [TCP on Netty at 300% faster than Netty alone](http://www.dzone.com/links/project_reactor_tcp_on_netty_is_300_faster_than_n.html)! When integrated into key Spring technologies, the possibilities of Fast Data are going to blow people's hair back. Congrats to [Jon Brisbin](https://twitter.com/j_brisbin)!
3.  Spring Data Arora Service Release 2 is available for [download](http://www.springsource.org/node/22654).
4.  Martin Lippert published an excellent blog on [Annotations and Java Config support](http://blog.springsource.org/2013/07/18/javaconfig-support-in-the-spring-tool-suite/) that are available in Spring Tool Suite 3.3.0. Support of JavaConfig as an XML alternative across the Spring ecosystem is nearing a pervasive level.
5.  Join Hemant Joshi as he introduces how to use Spring and the [Cucumber BDD testing framework in a webinar on July 30th, 2013](https://www.springsource.org/node/22644).
6.  Hadoop hungry? Join us for a Webinar series -- What You Can Do with Hadoop on the first Thursday of every month. The first webinar on August 1st, 2013 will provide in-depth details about the features and tutorials included in the Pivotal HD Single Node (VM).

7.  My buddy [Andy Piper (@andypiper)](http://twitter.com/andypiper) puts together a wonderful roundup of [Cloud Foundry](http://cloudfoundry.com/) called *[This Week in Cloud Foundry](http://www.thisweekincf.com/)*. I can't recommend it enough! He just started, and he's doing a heckuva job!
8.  The Zenika blog has a very nice post on how to document a REST API with [Swagger, which you can transparently layer on top of your Spring MVC API](http://blog.zenika.com/index.php?post/2013/07/11/Documenting-a-REST-API-with-Swagger-and-Spring-MVC).
9.  Matt Stine also has a great post on [Spring, Continuous Integration and CloudFoundry](http://www.mattstine.com/2013/07/10/blue-green-deployments-on-cloudfoundry/).
10.  The JavaCode Geeks blog has a nice post on how [to add validation to a REST API](http://www.javacodegeeks.com/2013/05/spring-from-the-trenches-adding-validation-to-a-rest-api.html)
11.  The `Pivotal` blog has a [*really* great post on how Tomcat compares to Pivotal's tcServer](http://blog.gopivotal.com/products/part-one-comparing-tomcat-and-pivotal-tc-server), a binary-compatible distribution of Tomcat that we support and augment for deployment
12.  *Also* on the `Pivotal` blog, a fantastic post on [how Spring Data GemFire (and GemFire) can *really* boost your application's performance](http://blog.gopivotal.com/products/run-spring-apps-60x-faster-with-spring-data-pivotal-gemfire)!
13.  Xavier Padró's has a really [nice introduction to messaging with Spring](http://xpadro.blogspot.com.es/2013/07/introduction-to-messaging-with-spring.html)
14.  This week at OSCON, I found affixed to all the bulletin boards and on the entry-doors into the conference a notice advertising a hackathon being run by [inBloom](http://inBloom.org), which is a nonprofit data and content services company working to support school districts as they implement great personalized learning tools for kids, teachers, and parents. inBloom is sponsoring a 2-day hackathon at OSCON to work on their open source content services. Check [out the projects](https://support.inbloom.org/secure/BrowseProjects.jspa#all) and the [code](https://github.com/inbloom)! I really enjoyed meeting these fine people and encourage any Spring ninjas out there to raise your hands and contribute!
