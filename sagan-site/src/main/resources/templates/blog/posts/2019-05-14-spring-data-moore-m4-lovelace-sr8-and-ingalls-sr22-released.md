# Spring Data Moore M4, Lovelace SR8 and Ingalls SR22 released

_Releases | Mark Paluch |  May 14, 2019 | 0 Comments_

I am pleased to announce the availability of Spring Data releases `Moore M4`, `Lovelace SR8`, and `Ingalls SR22`. Our releases build on the most recent [Spring Framework releases](https://spring.io/blog/2019/05/10/spring-framework-5-2-0-m2-5-1-7-5-0-14-and-4-3-24-available-now) and are going to be picked up by Spring Boot 2.2 M3, 2.1.5 and 1.5.21 respectively.

Moore M4 is also a pre-requisite for Spring Data R2DBC 1.0 M2. It ships with [70 tickets fixed](https://jira.spring.io/issues/?filter=16379). There are a few notable new features amongst these:

-   Support for reactive transaction management for MongoDB
-   Annotation-based Collation support for MongoDB
-   Reactive Index Operations in Elasticsearch and delete by query

Please find a high-level overview of what has been added in our [release wiki](https://github.com/spring-projects/spring-data-commons/wiki/Release-Train-Moore).

Due to a glitch in Lovelace SR7 and Ingalls SR22 released we immediately had to release Lovelace SR8 and Ingalls SR22 respectively. Lovelace SR7 and SR8 [fix 48 tickets](https://jira.spring.io/issues/?filter=16385) in total. Ingalls SR21 and SR22 ship with fixes for [31 tickets](https://jira.spring.io/issues/?filter=16387). Lovelace SR8 and Ingalls SR22 contain a fix for [CVE-2019-3802](https://pivotal.io/security/cve-2019-3802) and are highly recommended upgrades.

As always, we’re looking forward to your feedback. Leave us at Tweet at [@SpringData](https://twitter.com/SpringData). Finally, here's the laundry list for our releases:
