# Last chance to join 500+ others in Australia

_Engineering | Ben Alex |  November 01, 2006 | 0 Comments_

If you're reading this blog, chances are that you already know Spring is a pretty popular framework. Most J2EE developers who've ever used it simply love it, as illustrated by 12+ books, [1,000,000+](http://blog.interface21.com/main/2006/09/22/thank-you-spring-framework-passes-1-million-downloads/) [downloads](http://blog.interface21.com/main/2006/10/05/spring-20-final-with-over-10000-downloads-in-the-first-day/), 14,000+ [forum](http://forum.springframework.org/) members etc. Still, even I was surprised when my Australian Spring user group [announcement](http://lists.interface21.com/archives/australasia-spring-interest/2006-October/000002.html) a little over a week ago generated this much interest... As of today, we've had ***over five hundred*** registrations to attend these three meetings. Indeed, we've needed to move the [Sydney](http://tinyurl.com/yhtwe3) meeting to [larger premises](http://www.smc.au.com/rooms/grand.html), with the [Brisbane](http://tinyurl.com/yya6sz) and [Melbourne](http://tinyurl.com/swu24) meetings almost booked out. If you're located in Australia and would like to come along, I'd encourage you to register ASAP to avoid disappointment (don't forget to also subscribe to [Australasia Spring Interest](http://lists.interface21.com/listmanager/listinfo/australasia-spring-interest) to hear about future events).

Whilst on the topic of user group meetings, at the [last Sydney meeting](http://blog.interface21.com/main/2006/07/28/sydney-spring-user-group-meeting-2-planned/) I presented a session on using Spring 2.0 Namespaces. These are a fantastic new feature in Spring 2.0 that can be used to simplify your existing configuration - or even build your own problem domain-specific configuration schema. If you'd like to know more about this, here are [the slides](http://blog.interface21.com/main/wp-content/uploads/2006/11/Namespaces%20Presentation.pdf). You might also want to check out blog entries by [Ben Hale](http://blog.interface21.com/main/2006/08/28/creating-a-spring-20-namespace-use-springs-abstractbeandefintionparser-hierarchy/) and [Erik Wiersma](http://erik.jteam.nl/?p=27) on the same topic.
